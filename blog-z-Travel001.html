Good Bye Japan

  • Tomoya Machida
  • Life Decisions

Almost one month has past since I came to the US, and I’m getting used to culture and life, so now I think it’s time to share my journey. On September 22nd 2023, I landed in Seattle with many exceptions. I came here to change my career, perspective, and life. Let me explain why I move to here, I mean, why I move out from Japan.



Don’t get me wrong. I love Japan. I have so many things I can share about cool Japanese culture. I love Japanese food, I love Japanese culture, and I love my friends in Japan. Until 2023, I’ve never been abroad so there is no reason to dislike Japan obviously. But as I was getting to know foreign culture through taking with my friends, watching some news, I became to feel uncomfotable to live in Japan. So, I tried to figure out where the feeling comes from.

It took a time but I found reasons.

Expectations. As a Japanese, living in Japan means I need to live up to many expectations. I’m not sure if other Japanese feel in the same way but at least for me, yes. You have to get high grade in school, go to high ranked university, get a good job, work at same company, get married, have children. I actually thought It was my happiness and goals of my life until I got a job, but I felt this life is like following somebody’s happiness not mine. So, I needed a place where I don’t care about expectations.

New experience. I needed new experiences. I lived in Japan for 26 years and wherever I go, I couldn’t feel excitement as first experience. Even if it’s for the first time, I could relate it with my past experience. So, I needed a place where it’s has completely different view from Japan.

I think that’s all. But again, I love Japan, this is my country and I’m proud of being Japanese. I hope one day I can give back to Japan.

Thank you.
